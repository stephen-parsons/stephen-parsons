Blog entry #2

11/26/17

Hello world!

It’s the Sunday evening after Thanksgiving and I just now recall that I am due an entry. We had a short week at the Dojo and crammed in a whole lot in just 3 days. We were introduced to jQuery which is a LIFESAVER and makes modifying HTML a breeze, thus making any website more interactive and interesting. We all went over APIs and AJAX, which I find very interesting for some odd reason and I explored the chess.com API to make the Chess section on this website more informative. We start Python tomorrow, and I couldn’t be more excited. I’m a little weary of all the front-end dev and more interested in back-end and algorithms. I’ve done same basic Python and look forward to more exploring - I’m hoping to build a chess engine eventually and I think Python could be very useful tool for this project.

In other news, my family was in town and we had a nice Thanksgiving dinner in Bethesda before exploring the National Mall and all the monuments the next day. I am glad they could come by as it’s hard for me to leave town with Fox.

Besides that, the best part of last week was seeing Strike Anywhere! It’s refreshing to see a band that’s been around for awhile still deliver intensity and raw energy. Brain McTernan mentioned that you’d never meet a better group of guys and I believe him; their lyrics are meaningful on a level most other bands can’t touch. A reinvigorating experience that resets your perspective on playing music and really moves you.

On a related note I learned ‘Portrait of Authority’ by Bad Religion today. Another timeless band with exceptional lyrical prowess. This song in particular I sort of pass by most of the time, but the beautiful thing about Bad Religion is how you can come back to any song, examine it from a new perspective, learn something meaningful, and achieve an unprecedented cathartic release. A similar thing happened with ‘Infected’ and more recently ‘American Jesus’. Stunning masterworks that I simply overlooked because of their popularity in the BR canon.

Anyways, that’s all for now.

later,
Stephen

Blog entry #1

11/20/17

Hello world!

I have already completed week one of the CodingDojo, so I’ll start with a recap. I’m going to try and blog at the end of every week to record my coding journey.

The class is fantastic, and I can already tell that I’m picking up coding (at least WebFun) very quickly and easily and foresee a fruitful future. I absolutely loathe CSS and am currently enjoying the javaScript portion we started today far more. I will definitely update the CSS on here eventually… considering I achieved my Yellow Belt and can now code it properly. The exam was tedious, but not super difficult.

In other news I took out ‘Stranger Than Fiction’ for a spin today while working on some JS and I have to say it is definitely one of my top Bad Religion records. I hesitate to attach a number to it’s rank but the stylistic realization combined with higher budget production and overtly personal, but very dark, songwriting make it extremely powerful. This is the culmination of a long build-up in momentum for this band and I hold it in high regard in the BR pantheon.

Also, I’m going to see Strike Anywhere tomorrow at the 9:30 club!

later,
Stephen
